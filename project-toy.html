The Work

Bringing inclusive early childhood services into communities

TOY for Inclusion is an approach that expands access to inclusive, community-based early childhood education and care — bringing services directly to children and families rather than expecting them to navigate systems not designed with them in mind.

My Contribution

I designed the publication materials for the Virtual Pop-Up Museum, creating visual assets that communicated the initiative's community-centered approach across diverse audiences — from municipal partners and policymakers to families seeking inclusive early childhood services. The materials were presented during the project's closing meeting, attended by both practitioners and policymakers.

Design Approach

The challenge was to strike a balance between accessibility and institutional professionalism. The materials needed to reflect the warmth and inclusivity of the initiative's mission while maintaining the clarity and rigor required for engagement with stakeholders at multiple levels. This required careful attention to tone, visual identity, and audience needs throughout the design process.
